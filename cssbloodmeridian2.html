Blood Meridian: Frontier Survival Recipes

Inspired by Cormac McCarthy's Blood Meridian, or The Evening Redness in the West

1. Fried Horsemeat

Context: When rations run out, the Glanton gang butchers horses for sustenance.

Ingredients:

  • Horse flank meat
  • Salt (if available)
  • Lard or rendered fat (optional)

Preparation:

Slice meat thinly and fry on hot stones or in a pan over open flame. Season if salt is on hand.

2. Mesquite-Roasted Jackrabbit

Context: Hunted in the desert for lean protein and roasted using mesquite wood.

Ingredients:

  • 1 Jackrabbit
  • Mesquite branches (for fire and smoke)
  • Optional: Wild herbs

Preparation:

Skin and clean rabbit. Spit-roast over mesquite fire for 2 to 3 hours. Baste with any available fat. Eat with hands.

3. Cornmeal Mush

Context: Staple food in border villages and campsites.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cornmeal
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 tsp salt (if available)

Preparation:

Bring water to a boil. Slowly stir in cornmeal. Reduce heat and cook until thick, stirring frequently. Serve hot.

4. Tallow and Tortilla

Context: A simple, high-fat meal for energy on the trail.

Ingredients:

  • 2 corn tortillas
  • 2 tbsp rendered tallow

Preparation:

Warm tortillas on hot stones or fire. Spread with tallow and roll up. Eat immediately.

5. Scalped Goat Stew

Context: Borderland stew using goat meat, desert herbs, and cactus water.

Ingredients:

  • Goat meat (chopped)
  • Wild onions or cactus paddles
